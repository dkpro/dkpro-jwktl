===Verb===
<!--not an interjection: see the definitions below-->
{{en-verb|inf=to [[shut]] [[up]]|shuts up|shutting up|shut up|shut up}}

# {{transitive}} To [[close]] (a building) so that no one can enter.
# {{transitive}} To [[terminate]] (a business).
# {{transitive}} To [[enclose]] (a person, animal or thing) in a room or other place so that it cannot leave.
#* '''1886''', {{w|Robert Louis Stevenson}}, {{w|Strange Case Of Dr Jekyll And Mr Hyde}}
#*: "You know the doctor's ways, sir," replied Poole, "and how he '''shuts himself up'''. Well, he's '''shut up''' again in the cabinet; and I don't like it, sir--I wish I may die if I like it. Mr. Utterson, sir, I'm afraid."
# {{transitive}} To [[put]] (an object) in a secure enclosed place.
# {{transitive}} To stop (a person) from [[talk]]ing or (a person or thing) making noise.
# {{intransitive}} Of a person, to stop [[talk]]ing or (of a person or thing) making noise.<!--Note: "Shut up!" is not an interjection: it is just the imperative form of the verb in this sense.-->
#: ''You are talking so loud that I can't hear the music &mdash; would you mind '''shutting''' up?''
# {{context|intransitive|slang|used only in the [[imperative]]}} I don't believe it!, [[no way]]!

====Usage notes====
When used in the imperative to tell someone to be quiet or to stop making a noise, this expression is considered forceful or impolite. A neutral alternative is "be quiet".
