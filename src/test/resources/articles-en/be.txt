====Inflection====
* The verb {{term||be|lang=en}} is the most irregular non-defective verb in Standard English. Unlike other verbs, which distinguish at most five forms (as in {{term||do|lang=en}}â€“{{term||does|lang=en}}â€“{{term||doing|lang=en}}â€“{{term||did|lang=en}}â€“{{term||done|lang=en}}), {{term||be|lang=en}} distinguishes eight:
** {{term||Be|lang=en}} itself is the plain form, used as the infinitive, as the imperative, and as the present subjunctive.
**: ''I want to '''be''' a father someday.'' (infinitive)
**: ''That '''be''' the only reason.'' (infinitive)
**: ''Let the truth '''be''' heard!'' (infinitive)
**: ''Please '''be''' here by eight o'clock.'' (imperative)
**: ''The librarian asked that the rare books not '''be''' touched.'' (present subjunctive)
** {{term|am|Am|lang=en}}, {{term|are|lang=en}}, and {{term|is|lang=en}} are the forms of the present indicative. {{term||Am|lang=en}} is the first-person singular (used with {{term||I|lang=en}}); {{term||is|lang=en}} is the third-person singular (used with {{term||he|lang=en}}, {{term||she|lang=en}}, {{term||it|lang=en}} and other subjects that would be used with {{term||does|lang=en}} rather than {{term||do|lang=en}}); and {{term||are|lang=en}} is both the second-person singular and the plural (used with {{term||we|lang=en}}, {{term||you|lang=en}}, {{term||they|lang=en}}, and any other plural subjects).
**: '''''Am''' I in the right place?'' (first-person singular present indicative)
**: ''You '''are''' even taller than your brother!'' (second-person singular present indicative)
**: ''Where '''is''' the library?'' (third-person singular present indicative)
**: ''These '''are''' the biggest shoes we have.'' (plural present indicative)
** {{term|was|Was|lang=en}} and {{term|were|lang=en}} are the forms of the past indicative and past subjunctive (like {{term||did|lang=en}}). In the past indicative, {{term||was|lang=en}} is the firstâ€“ and third-person singular (used with {{term||I|lang=en}}, as well as with {{term||he|lang=en}}, {{term||she|lang=en}}, {{term||it|lang=en}} and other subjects that would be used with {{term||does|lang=en}} rather than {{term||do|lang=en}}), and {{term||were|lang=en}} is both the second-person singular and the plural (used with {{term||we|lang=en}}, {{term||you|lang=en}}, {{term||they|lang=en}}, and any other plural subjects). In the traditional past subjunctive, {{term||were|lang=en}} is used with all subjects, though many speakers do not actually distinguish the past subjunctive from the past indicative, and therefore use {{term||was|lang=en}} with firstâ€“ and third-person singular subjects even in cases where other speakers would use {{term||were|lang=en}}.
**: ''I '''was''' out of town.'' (first-person singular past indicative)
**: ''You '''were''' the first person here.'' (second-person singular past indicative)
**: ''The room '''was''' dirty.'' (third-person singular past indicative)
**: ''We '''were''' angry at each other.'' (plural past indicative)
**: ''I wish I '''were''' more sure.'' (first-person singular past subjunctive; ''was'' is also possible)
**: ''If she '''were''' here, she would know what to do.'' (third-person singular past subjunctive; ''was'' is also possible)
** {{term|being|Being|lang=en}} is the gerund-participle, used in noun-like constructions, in the progressive aspect, and after various verbs (like {{term||doing|lang=en}}). (It's also used as an actual noun; for those senses, see the entry for {{term|being|lang=en}} itself.)
**: ''I don't like '''being''' here.''
**: ''All of a sudden, he's '''being''' nice to everyone.''
**: ''It won't stop '''being''' a problem until someone does something about it.''
** {{term|been|Been|lang=en}} is the past participle, used in the perfect aspect.
**: ''It's '''been''' that way for a week and a half.''
* The forms {{term||am|lang=en}}, {{term||is|lang=en}}, and {{term||are|lang=en}} can contract with preceding subjects: {{term|I'm|Iâ€™m|I am|lang=en}}, {{term|'s|â€™s|is|lang=en}}, {{term|'re|â€™re|are|lang=en}}. **: ''I wonder what it '''is'''.''

* Several of {{term||be|lang=en}}â€™s finite forms have special negative forms, containing the suffix {{term|-n't|-nâ€™t|lang=en}}, that can be used instead of adding the adverb {{term|not|lang=en}}.
