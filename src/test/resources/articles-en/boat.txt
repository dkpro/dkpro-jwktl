===Etymology===
From {{etyl|enm}} {{term|boot|lang=enm}}, {{term|bot|lang=enm}}, {{term|boet|lang=enm}}, {{term|boyt||boatlang=enm}} < {{etyl|ang}} {{term|bat|bāt|boat|lang=ang}} < {{proto|Germanic|baitaz||baitan|boat, small ship|lang=en}} < {{proto|Indo-European|bheid-|to break, split|lang=en}}. Cognate with {{etyl|non|-}} {{term|beit||boat|lang=non}}.

{{etyl|non|-}} {{term|bátr|bātr|lang=non}} (whence {{etyl|is|-}} {{term|bátur|lang=is}}, {{etyl|no|-}} {{term|båt|lang=no}}),  {{etyl|nl|-}} {{term|boot|lang=nl}}, {{etyl|de|-}} {{term|Boot|lang=de}}, and {{etyl|fr|-}} {{term|bateau|lang=fr}} are all ultimately borrowings from the {{ang}} word.

===Noun===
{{en-noun}}

# A [[craft]] used for [[transportation]] of goods, fishing, racing, recreational cruising, or military use on or in the [[water]], [[propelled]] by [[oar]]s or [[outboard motor]] or inboard [[motor]] or by [[wind]].
# {{context|poker slang}} A [[full house]].
# {{chemistry}} One of two possible [[conformer]]s of [[cyclohexane]] rings (the other being [[chair]]), shaped roughly like a boat.
