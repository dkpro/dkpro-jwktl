===Etymology===
Transliteration of {{etyl|de}} {{term|X-Strahl|lang=de}}, coined by [[w:Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen|Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen]] upon his discovery of the rays in 1895, {{term|x}} signifying their [[unknown]] nature.

===Pronunciation===
* {{audio|en-us-X-ray.ogg|Audio (US)}}

===Noun===
{{en-noun|sg=[[X]]-[[ray]]}}

# Short [[wavelength]] [[electromagnetic]] [[radiation]] usually produced by [[bombard]]ing a metal target in a vacuum. Used to create [[image]]s of the [[internal]] structure of objects; this is possible because X-rays pass through most objects and can [[expose]] [[photographic]] [[film]].
#: '''''X-rays''' are light with a wavelength between 0.1 and 10 nm.''
# A [[radiograph]]: a [[photograph]] made with X-rays.
#: '' "The doctor ordered some '''X-rays''' of my injured wrist." ''
# An X-ray [[machine]].
# The letter ''X'' in the [[ICAO spelling alphabet]].
